  • Algebra is the branch of mathematics in which abstract symbols,rather than numbers are manipulated or operated with arithmetic.It is simply the part of mathematics that helps represent problems or situations in the form of mathematical expressions.

    • Branches of algebra-

    (i)Elementary algebra (ii)Advanced algebra (iii)Abstract algebra (iii)Abstract algebra (iv)Linear Algebra (v)Commutative algebra

    Algebraic Equations-An algebraic equation is an equation invoving only algebraic expressions in the unknowns.These are further classified by degree.

    (a) Linear equation-algebraic equation of degree one.

    (b) Polynomial equation-equation in which a polynomial is set equal to another polynomial.

    (c) Transcendental equation-equation involving a transcendental function of one of its variables.

    (d) Functional equatiom-equation in which the unknowns are ffunctions rather than simple quantities.

    (e) Differential equation-equation involving derivatives.

    (f) Integral equation-equation involving integrals.

    (g) Diophantine equation-equation where the only solutions of interest of the unknowns are the integer ones.

    General algebra concepts

    (i)Fundamental theorem of algebra-states that every non-constant single variable polynomial with complex coefficients has atleast one complex root.This includes polynomials with real coefficients,since every real number is a complex number with an imaginary part equal to zero.

    (ii)Equations-equality of two mathematical expressions.

    (iii)Linear equation-an algebraic equation of degree one.

    (iv)Quadratic equaation-an algebraic equation of degree two.

    (v)Cubic equation-an algebraic equation of degree three.

    (vi)Quartic equations-an algebraic equations of degree four.

    (vii)Quintic equations-an algebraic equations of degree five.

    (viii)Polynomial-an algebraic equation consisting of variables and polynomials.

    (ix)Inequalities-a comparision between values.

    (x)Functions-mapping that associates a single output value with each input value.

    (xi)Sequences-ordered list of elements either finite or infinite.

    (xii)Systems of equations-finite set of equations.

    (xiii)Vectors-element of a vector space.

    (xiv)Matrix-two dimensional array of numbers.

    (xv)Vector space-basic algebraic structure of linear algebra.

    (xvi)Field-algebraic structure with addition,multiplication and division.

    (xvii)Groups-algebraic structure with a single binary operation.

    (xviii)Rings-algebraic structure with addition and multiplication.

  • Associative property of addition:a+(b+c)=(a+b)+c

  • Associative property of multiplication:a × (b × c)=(a × b)× c
